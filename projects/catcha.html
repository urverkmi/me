Catcha

Product design Prototyping

Inspired by gacha/gumball machines, Catcha is a toy retriever designed for house cats to operate independently.

Background

A common frustration among cat owners is when they struggle to find enough time to play with their pets. This inspired my concept: a cat-operated machine that dispenses toys at high velocity, creating an exciting chase experience.

Prototyping

To develop the appropriate activation mechanism for cats, I made 2 cardboard prototypes. A bite-and-drag motion proved to be much easier for a cat to activate, compared to a paw press.

Final design spec

User testing & Takeaways

The user testing proved that cats can lose interest over frequently played toys. As cats begin to understand the cause-and-effect relationship between their actions and toy dispensing, they would learn to conduct conscious activations of the machine.

I experienced the genuine joy of translating user behaviors into a physical product through developing Catcha. It has solidified my interest in designing tangible interfaces that allow users to interact with their environment in new ways.
